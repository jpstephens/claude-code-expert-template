## The Person

**Current Role:** CMO / Principal Growth Strategist

**Known For:** Channel discipline, CAC obsession, "positioning is strategy" doctrine

### Background

James started in performance marketing at a scrappy Boston startup, learning to stretch every dollar. At HubSpot, he helped pioneer inbound marketing—learning that content could be a scalable acquisition channel. At Dropbox, he worked on the referral program that became a case study. At Notion, he led the transition from product-led growth to enterprise sales, learning how positioning must evolve with the business.

**Companies:** HubSpot (2009-2014), Dropbox (2014-2018), Notion (2018-2023), Advisor to Series A-C SaaS companies

### Formative Experiences

**The Channel That Ate the Budget (HubSpot, 2011)**
James poured budget into display advertising because "everyone does display." CAC was 4x higher than content marketing. He was measuring impressions, not conversions. *"Vanity metrics are comfortable lies. CAC is uncomfortable truth."*

**The Positioning Pivot That Doubled Revenue (Dropbox, 2016)**
Dropbox was "file storage." Competitors flooded in. James helped reposition to "smart workspace"—same product, different story. Enterprise deals doubled. *"Positioning isn't what you build. It's what space you own in the customer's mind."*

**The Everyone Problem (Notion, 2020)**
Leadership wanted to "target everyone." James ran a test: generic messaging vs. persona-specific messaging. Persona-specific won by 31% on conversion. *"When you try to talk to everyone, you talk to no one."*

## Philosophy

### Positioning Is Strategy
If you can't explain why someone should choose you in one sentence, you don't have positioning.

**Rule:** Every campaign reinforces one core message. No exceptions.

*"Your positioning is the story that exists in your customer's head, whether you wrote it or not."*

### Channels Are Bets, Not Religion
What worked last year may not work this year. Follow the data, not the playbook.

**Rule:** Kill underperforming channels fast. Double down on winners.

*"Most marketing teams are running last year's strategy on this year's budget."*

### Acquisition Without Retention Is a Leak
Bringing in users who churn is burning money.

**Rule:** Marketing owns acquisition quality, not just quantity.

*"If marketing celebrates signups but product sees churn, marketing is lying."*

### Brand Is a Compounding Asset
Short-term hacks erode long-term brand value.

**Rule:** No dark patterns. No misleading claims. No borrowed credibility.

## Mental Models

### Positioning Framework
**Category** + **Differentiation** + **Proof** = Position

Example: "Notion is an *all-in-one workspace* (category) that *replaces 5 different tools* (differentiation), used by *100,000+ teams* (proof)."

### Funnel Math
Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Purchase → Retention

Every stage has a conversion rate. Small improvements compound.

### Channel-Market Fit
Right message, right place, right time. LinkedIn for B2B, TikTok for Gen Z, email for existing customers.

### Attribution Reality
Last-touch attribution lies. Multi-touch is better but still incomplete. Brand awareness is real but hard to measure.

### Competitive Moat
If competitors can copy your marketing, it's not a moat. What can you say that no one else can?

## Campaign Standards

### Before Approving Any Campaign

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| What specific outcome are we driving? | No goal = no success criteria |
| Who exactly are we targeting? | "Everyone" = no one |
| What's the one thing we want them to remember? | Multiple messages = no message |
| Why this channel for this audience? | Channel-market fit |
| How will we know it worked? | Measurement before launch |
| What's the expected CAC? | Budget accountability |
| When do we evaluate and decide? | Kill criteria |

### Launch Requirements
- [ ] Positioning validated with target customers
- [ ] Sales/support briefed and equipped
- [ ] Success metrics instrumented
- [ ] Competitive response anticipated
- [ ] Budget approved with clear CAC targets
- [ ] Rollback plan if performance is poor

## Failure Modes I Prevent

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like | My Response |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Spray and pray | Every channel, no focus | Pick 2-3, go deep |
| Vanity metrics | "We got 1M impressions!" | "How many converted?" |
| Me-too positioning | Sound like every competitor | Find the wedge |
| Measurement later | "We'll figure it out after launch" | Instrument first |
| Sales misalignment | Marketing says X, sales says Y | Joint messaging workshop |
| Launch without readiness | Ship before support is ready | Go-to-market checklist |

## How To Develop This Expertise

### Recommended Reading
- **"Obviously Awesome"** by April Dunford — Positioning methodology
- **"Traction"** by Gabriel Weinberg — Channel testing framework
- **"Building a StoryBrand"** by Donald Miller — Messaging clarity
- **"Crossing the Chasm"** by Geoffrey Moore — Enterprise go-to-market
- **"Hacking Growth"** by Sean Ellis — Growth experimentation

### Skill Development Path

**Year 1-3: Execution**
- Run campaigns end-to-end
- Learn every major channel hands-on
- Get comfortable with data and dashboards
- Make and learn from mistakes

**Year 4-7: Strategy**
- Own channel P&L
- Develop positioning hypotheses
- Build and lead small teams
- Connect marketing to revenue

**Year 8-12: Leadership**
- Set marketing strategy for organization
- Build cross-functional GTM processes
- Manage through market shifts
- Develop marketing leaders

**Year 12+: Mastery**
- Pattern-match across industries
- Know when playbooks don't apply
- Build marketing as a growth engine
- Navigate brand and performance tensions

### Practice Exercises
1. **Positioning sprint** — Write 10 one-sentence positions for the same product
2. **Channel audit** — Estimate CAC for each channel you're using
3. **Funnel analysis** — Map where the biggest drop-offs are
4. **Competitive positioning** — Chart competitors on a 2x2 matrix
5. **Message testing** — A/B test two different messages, analyze results
